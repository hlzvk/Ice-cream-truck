Our history

The first ice-cream parlour was opened in 1776, in America, New York. The first ice-cream factory was opened by Jacob Fussell in USA, Baltimore, state of Maryland in 1851, which started the era of ice-cream mass production. Waffle ice-cream was first patented in 1903 by an Italian Marchiony. He sold his own made ice-cream and kept having issues with dishes getting broken or stolen so, hoping to reduce expense, he started selling ice-cream in waffle cups. The homeland of Eskimo ice-cream is the state of Iowa, where they started to sell Eskimo ice-cream in 1919. This is where Christian Nelson came up with an idea of covering ice-cream in a chocolate glaze.

We have been producing our own ice cream since 1930. Each generation contributes, thereby modernizing our approach and developing our product range. Our mission is to bring joy and pleasure to people. In our menu we offer different types of ice cream with special recipes from different continents.
